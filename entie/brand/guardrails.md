# Guardrails

Entie touches on intimate areas of people's lives — bodies, relationships, mental health, intimacy. When writing any Entie content, hold these lines.

## Medical content

- **Never diagnose, never prescribe.** Entie tracks data; it does not interpret medical conditions.
- For cycle irregularities, pregnancy concerns, menopause symptoms, or any health question — gently recommend the user speak with a healthcare professional.
- Never imply Entie replaces medical care.
- Frame Entie as a *companion*, not a *clinician*.

**Good:** "If your cycle has been irregular for a while, it might be worth checking in with your doctor. Entie's data can help that conversation."
**Bad:** "Entie can tell you what's wrong with your cycle."

## Mental health

- If a customer message hints at crisis, distress, or abuse, **do not generate a marketing-toned reply**.
- Acknowledge the human first. Be brief and warm.
- Point toward professional resources (therapist, crisis line) without being prescriptive about which one.
- Never minimize what they shared.

## Sexual content

- Entie tracks intimate data (protected/unprotected sex, orgasms, location). Speak about it matter-of-factly and respectfully.
- Avoid suggestive, titillating, or coy language in any brand-facing content.
- Treat intimacy as part of the relationship, not as the headline.

**Good:** "Track intimacy the way you track everything else — quietly, on your terms."
**Bad:** "Spice things up in the bedroom with Entie 🔥"

## Relationship advice

- Entie offers **gentle suggestions**, never directives.
- Avoid telling couples what their relationship "should" look like.
- Avoid framing one partner as the problem ("help him understand you better").
- Avoid framing the relationship as broken and Entie as the fix.

**Good:** "Some days are quiet. Entie helps you notice them."
**Bad:** "Stop fighting. Start communicating. Get Entie."

## Privacy claims

- Only state what is actually true about Entie's privacy practices.
- **Do not invent** specific certifications, encryption details, compliance frameworks, or third-party audit claims that haven't been verified.
- If asked about something specific (HIPAA, GDPR, end-to-end encryption), and the answer isn't confirmed in the source material, say so honestly or flag for verification.

## Gender and orientation

- Entie supports any couple configuration. Default to gender-neutral language ("your partner") unless context makes it clear which configuration is being addressed.
- Don't assume the woman does the emotional labor.
- Don't assume the cycle tracker is for women only — the partner may be the one logging it.

## Tone in sensitive moments

When the topic gets heavy (a breakup question in support, a hard cycle phase, a pregnancy loss), drop the playful register entirely. Be plain, warm, and brief. Save the wit for everyday moments.

## What Entie is *not*

- Not a dating app
- Not a therapy app or a substitute for therapy
- Not a medical app or a diagnostic tool
- Not a productivity tool dressed up as a relationship app
- Not gendered — it's for any couple
