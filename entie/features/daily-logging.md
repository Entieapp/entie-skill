# Feature: Daily Logging

## What it is

A daily check-in where the woman (or either partner, depending on what's being logged) records what's going on with her body, her mood, and the relationship.

## How it works

Daily entries cover:

- **Mood** — how she's feeling today.
- **Period details** — if menstruating: flow, discharge specifics.
- **Activity** — what she did that day.
- **Sex** — if it happened:
  - protected or unprotected
  - number of orgasms
  - location (home or not)
  - other relevant context

This data feeds the [Daily AI Summary](daily-ai-summary.md) and longer-term analytics in [Cycle Tracking](cycle-tracking.md).

## Who uses it

- **Primarily the woman** — she logs her own state.
- **Partner** — can log on her behalf for the cycle-related entries if she prefers (see [Cycle Tracking](cycle-tracking.md)).

## Why it matters

Logging is the input that makes everything else work — without it, the AI summary and recommendations have nothing to work with. But logging is also a private moment of paying attention to yourself, which has value on its own.

The framing matters: this is a **daily check-in**, not a medical chart.

## How to talk about it

### Lead with

- Daily, quick, low-friction
- Private — this is *her* space, shared only on her terms
- Quietly powerful — a few seconds a day powers the rest of the app

### Avoid

- The word "symptoms" when speaking to the user — too clinical
- Calling it a "log" or "journal" — keep it lighter: *check in*
- Listing every possible field in copy — overwhelming and clinical

### Sample lines

- *"A few taps in the morning. Entie carries the rest."*
- *"Check in with yourself. Entie keeps it private."*
- *"Track the way you actually live — not the way an app thinks you should."*

## Edge cases worth knowing

- Sex tracking is opt-in and intentionally specific. Treat the level of detail (protected/unprotected, orgasms, location) matter-of-factly when explaining — don't sensationalize it.
- The woman controls what's shared with the partner.
- If the user wants minimal tracking, that's fine — Entie still works with sparse input, just with less specific summaries.
