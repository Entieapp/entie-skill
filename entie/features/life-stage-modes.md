# Feature: Life Stage Modes

## What it is

Beyond standard cycle tracking, Entie adapts to different life stages. The woman can switch the app into one of three modes, and the entire experience — tracking, recommendations, partner guidance — adapts accordingly.

## The three modes

### Cycle mode (default)

- The default menstrual cycle tracker
- See [`cycle-tracking.md`](cycle-tracking.md) for full details
- Use when the woman is menstruating and tracking her cycle

### Pregnancy mode

- Entie shifts its tracking and recommendations to support pregnancy
- The partner gets guidance appropriate for pregnancy stages, not cycle stages
- Daily summaries reflect pregnancy context

### Menopause mode

- Entie recognizes this stage of life and tailors guidance for both partners
- Tracking focuses on what matters in menopause rather than cycle prediction
- Partner guidance shifts to reflect the realities of this stage

## How it works

- The woman selects the mode that matches her current life stage.
- The app reconfigures: tracking inputs, AI summaries, partner recommendations, and analytics all adjust.
- The mode is set by the woman — she controls which stage she's in.

## Who uses it

- **Woman** — selects and changes the mode.
- **Partner** — receives stage-appropriate guidance based on the selected mode.

## Why it matters

A couple's life isn't a single phase. The same couple may go through pregnancy and later through menopause. An app that only handles one stage stops being useful at the other stages. Entie stays with the couple through transitions, adapting its guidance instead of becoming irrelevant.

This also signals respect for the full arc of a relationship — not just the years when fertility tracking matters.

## How to talk about it

### Lead with

- The app *changes with you*. Entie is for the long haul.
- Each stage has its own context — Entie speaks to that context, not a generic version.
- The partner gets stage-appropriate guidance, not one-size-fits-all tips.

### Avoid

- Treating pregnancy or menopause as edge cases — they're first-class modes
- Implying any stage is "better" or more central than another
- Medical or clinical framing — Entie supports through these stages, it doesn't manage them medically (see [`../brand/guardrails.md`](../brand/guardrails.md))

### Sample lines

- *"Cycle. Pregnancy. Menopause. Same Entie, ready for each one."*
- *"Your life changes. So does Entie."*
- *"Whatever stage you're in, your partner gets the right cues."*

## Edge cases worth knowing

- Transitions between modes (e.g., starting a pregnancy, entering menopause) should feel smooth — never describe them as "switching to a different app."
- Menopause is often underserved by tracking apps. Lean into the fact that Entie *includes* it as a first-class mode.
- Mode selection is the woman's choice. The partner should never be the one to set or change it.
