# Feature: Partner Connection

## What it is

The mechanism that links two partners' accounts inside Entie. The app understands that two specific people are romantic partners, which makes every shared feature possible.

## How it works

- Each partner has their own Entie account.
- The two accounts are linked through a connection step inside the app.
- Once connected, Entie recognizes them as a couple.
- All shared features flow through this connection:
  - [Couple Questions](couple-questions.md)
  - [Guided Space](ai-chat.md) (in AI Chat)
  - Shared cycle data
  - Shared [Daily AI Summary](daily-ai-summary.md)

## Supported couple configurations

Entie supports any couple configuration:

- Male–female
- Male–male
- Female–female

The partner connection is identical across configurations. Some features (cycle tracking, life stage modes) are tied to the partner who menstruates or is going through pregnancy/menopause — but the *connection* itself is configuration-agnostic.

## Who uses it

- **Both partners** — both must opt in for the connection to exist.

## Why it matters

Most relationship apps treat the user as a solo unit. Entie's whole shape depends on the two-account connection — without it, the shared features don't make sense. The connection is also the moment a couple commits to using Entie together, which is meaningful in itself.

## How to talk about it

### Lead with

- Two accounts, one couple — both partners need to be in
- The features that depend on it (Couple Questions, Guided Space, shared cycle data)
- Configuration-neutral language

### Avoid

- "Pair your phone" or "device pairing" — sounds technical and small
- Defaulting to male-female examples
- Implying one partner is the "primary" account

### Sample lines

- *"Two accounts. One shared space."*
- *"Connect with your partner. Then Entie can do its real work."*
- *"Any couple, any configuration."*

## Edge cases worth knowing

- If only one partner uses Entie, the solo features still work (cycle tracking, Simple Chat, Deep Talk, Daily Logging) — but shared features (Couple Questions, Guided Space, shared summaries) require the connection.
- Disconnection should be possible if the relationship ends. Treat this gently in support content — it's a real moment in people's lives.
- Privacy boundaries between connected accounts are real: the woman controls what data she shares with her partner.
