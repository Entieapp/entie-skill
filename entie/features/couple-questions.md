# Feature: Couple Questions

## What it is

Entie asks the couple a question. Both partners answer independently. Answers are only revealed once both have responded.

## How it works

- Entie sends a question to the couple.
- Each partner answers privately, without seeing the other's response.
- **Answers are revealed to both only when both have responded.** If only one answers, the other's reply stays hidden.
- Once both reveal, the app moves to the next question.
- Entie has a curated bank of questions of its own.
- **Either partner can submit a custom question**, and Entie finds the ideal moment to ask it.

## Who uses it

- **Both partners** — the feature only works when both engage.

## Why it matters

There are things couples don't say out loud — not because they're hiding them, but because there's no natural moment to bring them up. Couple Questions creates that moment without putting anyone on the spot. Because answers are only revealed when both have responded, neither partner risks being vulnerable alone.

The custom-question feature is quietly powerful: a partner can ask the thing they've been wanting to ask, and Entie picks the right time to deliver it.

## How to talk about it

### Lead with

- The synced-reveal mechanic — this is the differentiator and the magic
- The safety of answering first, seeing later
- The option to submit your own question

### Avoid

- Comparing it to a "game" — it's more intimate than that
- "Quizzes" or "trivia" language
- Implying it's about scoring or matching

### Sample lines

- *"Both answer first. Then both see."*
- *"Ask the thing you've been meaning to ask. Entie picks the moment."*
- *"A question a day. No pressure to answer alone."*

## Edge cases worth knowing

- If only one partner ever answers, the answers never reveal. That's the design, not a bug.
- The "ideal moment" for a custom question is determined by Entie based on context (cycle day, mood, recent activity, etc.) — don't promise a specific timing.
- Sensitive custom questions still need to land safely. The feature isn't a vehicle for ambush — frame it as care.
