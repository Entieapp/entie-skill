# Feature: Cycle Tracking

## What it is

A full menstrual cycle tracker built for couples. Either partner can log entries. The woman gets cycle analytics; the partner gets daily guidance on how to support her.

## How it works

- The woman can log her period, see when the next one is expected, and view analytics on her cycle.
- Analytics cover: regularity vs. irregularity of the cycle itself, regularity of period length, and any deviations.
- **The partner can also log her cycle on her behalf** — either as a backup (if she doesn't keep a calendar herself) or as a shared responsibility.
- The partner sees the same cycle data she does, *plus* daily guidance on how to show up for her at this stage of her cycle.
- Every day, the partner gets fresh recommendations tailored to where she is in the cycle.

## Who uses it

- **Woman** — tracks her cycle, sees analytics, controls what's shared.
- **Partner** — can log on her behalf, sees the cycle data, gets daily support tips.
- **Both** — share awareness of the cycle as part of their shared life.

## Why it matters

Most cycle apps assume tracking is a solo female task. That assumption leaves the partner clueless and the woman doing all the work of remembering. Entie removes that assumption: tracking becomes a shared act of care, and the partner finally has something specific and helpful to do.

This also serves women who *don't* want to track but whose partners do want to know — the partner can carry it.

## How to talk about it

### Lead with

- The fact that **either partner can log it**. This is the differentiator.
- The daily support guidance for the partner.
- Shared awareness as an expression of care.

### Avoid

- Clinical language ("symptoms," "patient," "diagnostic")
- Framing the cycle as a problem to manage
- Anything that sounds like the man is "fixing" the woman
- Calling it just a "period tracker" — it's bigger than that

### Sample lines

- *"She tracks. He tracks. Either way, no one has to remember alone."*
- *"On day 23 he gets a heads-up. On day 1 he already knows."*
- *"Cycle data, daily support, shared between you."*

## Edge cases worth knowing

- The woman may choose not to share certain data with the partner — privacy choices live with her.
- If the woman is in **Pregnancy** or **Menopause** mode (see [`life-stage-modes.md`](life-stage-modes.md)), cycle tracking adapts accordingly.
