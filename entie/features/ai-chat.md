# Feature: AI Chat — Three Modes

The in-app AI chat has three distinct modes. Each one serves a different need.

## Mode 1: Simple Chat

### What it is

A standard question-and-answer chat with the AI. Ask anything about your relationship and get a response.

### When to use it (for the user)

- Quick questions
- Needing a second opinion in the moment
- Curiosity, not crisis

### How to talk about it

- "Ask Entie anything"
- Keep it casual — this is the everyday version
- Don't oversell it as therapy or coaching

## Mode 2: Deep Talk

### What it is

A guided mode where the AI asks the user a series of questions. Once the user has answered, the AI offers **three possible interpretations or scenarios** for the situation, each rated by how likely it is.

### How it works

1. User starts a Deep Talk.
2. AI asks a series of questions to understand the situation.
3. User answers.
4. AI returns three scenarios — possible readings of what's going on — with a probability rating for each.

### When to use it (for the user)

- Working through a confusing moment alone
- When you're not sure what just happened in an interaction
- Pre-conversation prep ("what's actually going on here?")

### How to talk about it

- "Three takes on what might be going on. You decide which one fits."
- Emphasize the *three scenarios with probabilities* — it's the signature of this mode
- Frame it as thinking *with* an AI, not being told what to think

### Sample lines

- *"Stuck on something? Deep Talk it out."*
- *"Three possible reads on the moment. Yours to pick."*

## Mode 3: Guided Space

### What it is

Both partners are in the same chat together, talking to each other. **The AI is in the room too** — listening, gently flagging what's off, surfacing things the partners might be missing, and offering suggestions in real time.

### How it works

- Both partners enter the chat.
- They talk to each other normally.
- The AI participates as a third presence: it notices patterns, calls out things one partner may have missed, and offers gentle suggestions.

### When to use it (for the user)

- Hard conversations
- When you keep going in circles on the same topic
- When one partner feels unheard
- Anytime a third perspective would help

### How to talk about it

- **This is the flagship mode.** Lead with it in marketing when AI Chat is the focus.
- It's couples-therapy-adjacent — but private, on-demand, and on the couple's terms.
- Avoid the word "mediator" — sounds adversarial.
- Avoid "AI therapist" — Entie is not a therapist (see [`../brand/guardrails.md`](../brand/guardrails.md)).

### Sample lines

- *"You. Your partner. A quiet third presence that catches what you miss."*
- *"The hard conversation, with help in the room."*
- *"On your terms. In your time. Just the three of you."*

## Who uses it

- **Simple Chat** — either partner, solo
- **Deep Talk** — either partner, solo
- **Guided Space** — both partners, together

## Why these three modes matter

Couples need different kinds of help in different moments. A quick question isn't the same as untangling a confusing situation, which isn't the same as having a hard talk together. The three modes map to those three needs without forcing one tool to do all three jobs.

## How to talk about all three together

When explaining the chat as a whole, name the modes by their actual names so users build vocabulary: **Simple Chat, Deep Talk, Guided Space.** Don't rename them or use clever alternatives.
