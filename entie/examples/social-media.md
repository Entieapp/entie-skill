# Social Media Examples

Short-form copy for Instagram, TikTok, X, and similar. Pick the format you're writing for and match the register.

## Voice on social

- A touch more playful than the App Store
- Still warm, never snarky at couples' expense
- Real moments beat abstract claims
- Avoid heavy hashtag stacks — 1–3 relevant tags max

## Instagram captions

### Carousel post (intro to Entie)
> Most relationship apps want you to *do more*.
>
> Entie just wants you to notice.
>
> Tap through →

### Single image (cycle feature)
> Day 23. He gets a heads-up.
> Day 1. He already knows.
>
> Cycle tracking, but built for two.

### Reel caption (Guided Space)
> The hard conversation, with a quiet third presence in the room.
> Just the three of you. On your terms.

### Story prompt
> "What's one thing you've been meaning to ask your partner?"
>
> Tap to ask it — Entie picks the right moment.

## X / Twitter

Short, observational. One idea per post.

> the romance is real
> the laundry is also real
> entie is for the second one
> so you can enjoy the first one

> we built a cycle tracker
> that either of you can use
> because remembering shouldn't be one person's job

> guided space is what happens
> when "we need to talk" finally happens

## TikTok hook lines

For voiceover or on-screen text. Hooks should land in the first 2 seconds.

- "POV: your partner's cycle tracker is also yours now"
- "I asked an AI to mediate our argument. Here's what happened."
- "Three things Entie noticed about my relationship that I didn't"
- "The relationship app that doesn't make you do homework"

## Push notifications

Short, specific, never naggy.

### Good
- *"Day 1 of her cycle. Here's a small thing that might help."*
- *"She just answered today's question. Your turn."*
- *"Quiet evening today, based on what she logged this morning."*

### Bad
- ❌ "You have 1 unread insight!" (gamified, generic)
- ❌ "Don't forget to track today! 💕" (naggy + emoji-heavy)
- ❌ "Improve your relationship with Entie." (marketing, not utility)

## What to avoid on social

- Generic relationship tropes ("date night ideas!", "5 love languages")
- Therapy-speak ("attachment-aware app", "regulate your nervous system")
- Couple-shaming setups ("if your partner doesn't do X, they don't love you")
- Anything that frames one gender as the problem
