# Customer Support Reply Examples

Support replies should be warm and personable first; correct or instruct second. Always use the customer's name if known.

## General principles

- Open with acknowledgment, not the answer.
- One question, one answer. Don't bury the response in caveats.
- Anticipate the next question if it's obvious.
- Never get defensive about privacy — explain plainly.
- Drop the playful register in heavy moments. Be plain and warm.
- See [`../brand/guardrails.md`](../brand/guardrails.md) for medical and mental health rules.

## Example 1 — feature question

**Customer:** "Can my husband also track my period in the app, or just me?"

**Reply:**
> Hi Sarah,
>
> Yes — either of you can log your cycle in Entie. A lot of couples share it this way: sometimes you do it, sometimes he does. Whichever of you logs the entry, both of you see the same cycle data, and he gets daily tips on how to show up for you at each stage.
>
> You stay in control of what you share, though — that part doesn't change.
>
> — The Entie team

## Example 2 — privacy concern

**Customer:** "I'm a little uncomfortable putting this much personal data in an app. How do you actually protect it?"

**Reply:**
> Hi Alex,
>
> That's a fair question, and we'd rather you ask it than not. Privacy is a core part of how we built Entie — your data belongs to you, and you control what your partner can and can't see inside the app. We don't sell data, and we treat the intimate stuff (cycle, sex, mood logs) with the same care we'd want for our own.
>
> If you want the specifics on how data is stored and secured, [link to privacy policy] has the full picture.
>
> Happy to answer anything else.
>
> — The Entie team

*Note: don't invent specific certifications or encryption claims that haven't been verified. Keep it to what's true. See [`../brand/guardrails.md`](../brand/guardrails.md).*

## Example 3 — disappointed user

**Customer:** "I've been using Entie for a month and honestly we haven't talked any more than before. Disappointed."

**Reply:**
> Hi Jamie,
>
> Thanks for telling us — that's the kind of honesty we want.
>
> Entie isn't a magic switch, and a month is short. But it's also fair feedback that something's not landing for you. A couple of questions that might help us figure out what:
>
> — Have either of you tried Guided Space yet? It's the mode designed for the harder conversations.
> — Are you both opening the daily summary regularly?
>
> If you want to share a little more about what you were hoping for, we'll see if we can point you somewhere useful — or pass it along to the product team if Entie's just not doing what you need.
>
> — The Entie team

## Example 4 — medical question

**Customer:** "My cycle has been irregular for three months. What does Entie say is wrong?"

**Reply:**
> Hi Maria,
>
> We want to be honest with you here — Entie tracks and surfaces patterns, but we're not a medical service, so we can't tell you what's behind an irregularity. Three months is the kind of thing that's worth talking to a doctor about. Your Entie data can actually be useful in that conversation — you can show them exactly what you've been seeing.
>
> If there's anything we can do to make that easier (exporting your data, etc.), let us know.
>
> — The Entie team

## Example 5 — heavy moment

**Customer:** "I think I need to delete the app. We broke up last night."

**Reply:**
> Hi Chris,
>
> We're sorry. That's a hard thing to write.
>
> Whenever you're ready, you can disconnect from your partner inside the app or delete your account entirely — settings → account. No rush.
>
> Take care.
>
> — The Entie team

*Note: short, plain, no marketing tone. Don't try to retain them. Don't add a CTA.*
